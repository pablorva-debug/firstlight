Honest answers

Questions parents actually ask.

Is this therapy?

No. Coaching is forward-looking and practical. We're not exploring your childhood — we're building a calmer, more confident week ahead. If I ever feel therapy would serve you better, I'll say so honestly.

Is online coaching as effective as in person?

Yes — and for most families, more convenient. Research consistently shows video coaching is just as effective as face-to-face. You join from your own home with your child nearby, no babysitter or travel needed. The vast majority of my clients work with me entirely online and see exactly the same results.

What actually happens on the free 20-minute call?

I'll ask a few simple questions about what's feeling hardest and about your child's age and situation. There's no script, no assessment, and genuinely no sales pitch. At the end I'll tell you honestly whether I think I can help, and which session would be the best fit. You are not committing to anything by booking it.

My baby is four months old. Too early?

Honestly, it's the best time. The patterns you set in the first year are the ones you'll be grateful for — or carefully unwinding — at age two. New-parent coaching is some of the most impactful work I do.

Do I need to come with my partner?

Not at all. About half my clients come solo, half as a couple. Many find it easiest to start alone and bring their partner in once there's a clear direction to share. The Partner Session is specifically for couples who want to tackle disagreements together from the start.

Really — English, French and Spanish?

Really. I work fluently across all three, which matters enormously when you're parenting in a language that isn't your mother tongue. We can even mix languages within a session if that's more natural for you.

What if it doesn't feel right for us?

If after the first paid session you don't feel this is the right fit, I'll refund it in full. No awkwardness, no questions asked.
